# Pricing And Gating Product Map

## Product thesis

Zania should be free at the point of discovery and draft planning, then paid at the point where a wedding becomes collaborative, coordinated, and operationally active.

That means the commercial model should separate:

- exploration from execution
- solo planning from shared planning
- wedding-owned collaboration from professional subscriptions

## Commercial model by audience

### Couples

The couple product is wedding-owned.

That means:

- the wedding is the billable workspace
- the couple owns the wedding
- collaboration, AI, and add-ons unlock at the wedding level

#### Free

- account creation
- cost estimator
- browse planners and vendors
- shortlist vendors
- basic budget, tasks, and guest planning

#### Basic

- planner collaboration
- vendor collaboration
- committee collaboration up to 10 people
- family collaboration up to 10 people

#### Premium

- everything in Basic
- AI Wedding Assistant
- timeline management
- expanded committee collaboration up to 20 people
- expanded family collaboration up to 20 people
- richer planner and vendor coordination

#### Add-ons

- Gift Registry
- Guest RSVP & Management

Pricing shape:

- monthly or annual per wedding
- add-ons attach to the wedding workspace

### Wedding committees

Committee participation is not sold as a separate primary couple-facing plan.

Instead:

- committee members collaborate inside the couple-owned wedding workspace
- committee access is unlocked by the wedding's couple plan
- committee capacity is governed by wedding-level collaboration seat limits

Free:

- can exist as a draft or backfilled workspace during migration
- can browse vendors and planners
- can contribute only when invited into an entitled wedding workspace

Paid access:

- unlocked through the wedding's Basic or Premium plan
- Basic unlocks shared committee collaboration
- Premium unlocks AI and timeline-driven execution tooling

Pricing shape:

- bundled under the wedding's couple plan

### Professional planners

Free:

- create planner profile
- manage 1 active wedding
- use core planner workflow

Paid: `Planner Pro`

- multiple active weddings
- exports
- planner reporting
- calendar sync
- richer collaboration tooling

Pricing shape:

- monthly or annual subscription

Important rule:

- gate planners by active weddings, not lifetime weddings

### Vendors

Free:

- create listing
- directory visibility
- manage portfolio and profile

Paid: `Vendor Pro`

- receive direct connection requests
- respond to leads
- analytics and performance insights
- premium visibility and growth tools

Pricing shape:

- monthly subscription

## Pricing page structure

The pricing page should explain the model in three layers:

### 1. Hero

Message:

- start free
- upgrade when you're ready to plan together
- upgrade again when you want a fully coordinated wedding workspace

Primary CTA:

- create account

Secondary CTA:

- back to homepage

### 2. Couple plan ladder

Show three primary couple cards:

- Free
- Basic
- Premium

Then show add-ons underneath:

- Gift Registry
- Guest RSVP & Management

### 3. Professional cards

Separate cards for:

- Professional Planners
- Vendors

Each card should show:

- pricing model
- free tier summary
- paid tier summary
- common upgrade triggers

### 4. Feature gate map

Show the exact product rules per audience:

- what is included free
- what unlocks after upgrade
- which features are sold as add-ons

### 5. Access-control rollout section

Explain the implementation sequence for subscriptions and entitlements:

- wedding-level entitlements for couples
- audience-specific plans for planners and vendors
- upgrade triggers at action points
- future billing integration

## Exact gated feature map

### Couples

#### Free

- estimator
- directory browsing
- shortlists
- draft budget
- draft tasks
- draft guests
- vendor management

#### Basic

- connect with vendors
- connect with planners
- collaborate with committee and family
- shared planning workspace up to 10 committee seats and 10 family seats

#### Premium

- AI Wedding Assistant
- timeline management
- fuller wedding coordination across planner, vendors, and collaborators
- up to 20 committee seats and 20 family seats

#### Add-ons

- Gift Registry
- Guest RSVP & Management

Upgrade triggers:

- connect to a vendor
- connect to a planner
- invite collaborators
- exceed free solo planning into shared planning
- open AI assistant
- unlock collaborative timeline
- enable gift registry or RSVP workflows

### Wedding committees

Committee members inherit access from the couple's wedding plan.

#### Included through Basic or Premium

- participate in shared tasks and collaboration
- contribute inside the wedding workspace

#### Premium-only committee experience

- AI-assisted coordination
- advanced timeline-driven execution

Upgrade triggers:

- invite committee members into a free wedding
- exceed committee seat limits
- open AI or timeline management through committee workflow

### Professional planners

Free:

- planner profile
- 1 active wedding
- core planning workflow

Paid:

- more than 1 active wedding
- exports
- planner reporting
- calendar sync
- richer client collaboration

Upgrade triggers:

- add a second active wedding
- export client progress
- use advanced reporting

### Vendors

Free:

- basic listing
- portfolio and profile edits
- directory presence

Paid:

- direct lead access
- respond to leads
- analytics
- premium visibility

Upgrade triggers:

- receive/respond to direct leads
- open analytics
- unlock premium visibility

## Product wording

Use this framing consistently:

- `Free`: Plan your wedding
- `Basic`: Plan together
- `Premium`: Run the whole wedding in one place
- `Add-ons`: Extend your wedding workspace when you need more
- `Upgrade when you're ready to actively coordinate`
- `Upgrade when your business needs scale`

Avoid this framing:

- paywalling the whole product up front
- locking discovery before users understand the value
- using legacy `Planning Pass` language as the primary couple-facing label

## Recommended rollout

### Phase 1

- show upgrade prompts only
- do not enforce real payments yet

### Phase 2

- launch pricing page
- launch role-aware upgrade prompts
- align couple copy to Free / Basic / Premium

### Phase 3

- connect billing
- enforce wedding-level entitlements for couples

### Phase 4

- add reporting on conversion by trigger point
